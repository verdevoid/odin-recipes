Sinigang na Baboy (Pork Sinigang)

Description

Sinigang na baboy, or pork sinigang is one of the most known Filipino dishes. It's a soup with a tamarind-flavored broth, either pork ribs or pork belly, and vegetables, most commonly snake beans, tomatoes, water spinach, okra, daikon raddish, and tamarind. This hearty soup is perfect for cold weather, and my family and I usually dip the meat in patis, a fish sauce, with chilis for a salty, spicy kick.

This recipe uses pork belly rather than ribs, and branded mixes rather than grinding tamarind. The measurements for the ingredients is enough to serve 6 people.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. pork belly sliced into cubes
  • 2 to 3 cups kangkong (water spinach) leaves
  • 3 pieces siling haba (long green chili pepper)
  • 2 pieces tomato quartered
  • 1 cup sliced daikon radish labanos
  • 10 to 12 pieces snake beans cut in 2 inch length pieces
  • 1 piece onion quartered
  • 15 pieces okra
  • 2 pieces taro peeled and cut in half
  • 8 cups water
  • 2 packs Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc Mix Original (20 gram pack)
  • 3 tablespoons patis (fish sauce)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Pour water into a cooking pot. Let boil.
  2. Add onions and tomatoes. Boil for 5 to 8 minutes.
  3. Put the pork in the cooking pot. Boil for 25 minutes.
  4. Add the taro. Cover and continue to boil for 30 minutes.
  5. Add the Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc mix, fish sauce, and ground black pepper; stir.
  6. Put the daikon radish in. Cook for 3 to 5 minutes.
  7. Add the chili peppers, okra, and snake beans. Cook for 7 minutes.
  8. Add the kangkong or water spinach. Cover the cooking pot and turn off the heat. Let it stay covered for 5 minutes.
  9. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve with a small bowl with patis fish sauce and spicy chili.
